      <header class='bold'>Body Building and Warm Ups</header>
      <p>Both warm-ups and bodybuilding exercises are important for MMA training, but they serve different purposes. 
        <p>Warm-ups are crucial for preparing the body for the demands of training, improving flexibility, and preventing injuries.</p>
        <p>Bodybuilding, or strength training, can be beneficial for building muscle mass and increasing overall strength, which can be helpful for MMA, but it's not the primary focus.</p>
    <p>A well-rounded MMA training program should incorporate both</p>
      <p>Types of Exercises: Dynamic stretches (arm circles, leg swings, torso twists), light cardio (jogging, shadow boxing), and mobility drills. 
</p>

    <section class="main-section" id="About_20_Blocks">
      <header class='bold'>About 20 Blocks</header>
      <p>
Here are 20 block names relevant to Mixed Martial Arts (MMA), categorized for clarity:
</p>
      <ol>
        <p class='italic'> Basic Blocks</p>
        <li>High Block: Protects the head and face from strikes.</li>
        <li>Low Block: Defends against kicks to the legs and body shots.</li>
<li>Lead Hand Guard: Using the lead arm to protect the head and body.</li>
<li>Rear Hand Guard: Similar to the lead hand guard, but with the rear arm.</li>
<li>Double Forearm Block: Both forearms are used to block incoming strikes.</li>
<li>Clinch Block: Used to defend against strikes while clinching</li>

      </ol>
      <p class='italic'>Advanced Blocks</p>
      <ol>
        <li>Catch (or Catching): A technique to grab or deflect an incoming strike with the hand.</li>
        <li>Parry: A quick, deflected block to redirect an attack.</li>
        <li>Shoulder Block: Using the shoulder to deflect strikes.</li>
        <li>Elbow Block: Using the elbow to block strikes, often against punches.</li>
        <li>Knee Block: Using the knee to block kicks.</li>
        
      </ol>
      <p class='italic'>Blocks in Combination:</p>
      <ol>
        <li>Slip and Block: Slipping the head to evade a strike and then blocking with the opposite hand.</li>
        <li>Step Back and Block: Moving backward while blocking to create distance.</li>
        <li>Angle and Block: Moving to the side (angling) while blocking.</li>
        <li>Check (or Checking): A type of block used specifically against leg kicks.
</li>
        </ol>
        <p class='italic'>Defensive Techniques:</p>
        <ol>
          <li>Head Movement: Evasive movements like bobbing and weaving.</li>
            <li>Footwork: Moving in and out of range to avoid strikes.</li>
            <li>Feinting: A deceptive movement to draw out a reaction.</li>
            <li>Cover Up: Protecting the head and body with arms and gloves.</li>
            <li>Takedown Defense: Blocks and movements to prevent being taken down. 
</li>
  </ol>

    </section>
